Overview

Tackole is a food app for a restaurant that serves a variety of different foods for everyone’s desire, in the app users can order food, book a table in the restaurant, schedule preorders, and more.

The app also offers different experiences by providing new features made especially for people that spend most of their time working so they can feel they have a restaurant in their pockets.

Challenge

Provide a quick and easy way for busy users to simply one-click order their food with no effort, and also organize their meals schedule so they don’t miss up on their diet.

Also, allow small to medium companies to order big quantities of orders at the same time with the lowest mistakes possible.

Solution

Based on our research we found that the best way to achieve our goal is designing a mobile app that enables users to quickly order from just the home page to reduce the steps as much as possible. The whole app buttons architecture is made in a way to allow users to one-click-order most dishes and meals.

Also, adding an order schedule allows users to plan their daily order meals for a week ahead so they don’t miss up on their diet.

📄User research

Project plan

We started by doing qualitative user research by doing one-to-one interviews through online meeting rooms, participants were given specific tasks to do, and we kept observing their behavior throughout the interview sessions.

Next, we did surveys with much more participants, we collected both data and analyzed them to put them side by side with our assumptions.

Some of our assumptions that we were confident in were wrong according to our user-collected data, for example, we found that users prefer to order the same food they like over trying something new every time and that’s why we add the “favorite” section in the home page.

Qualitative User Research

After conducting user one-to-one user interviews with 10 participants we gathered enough raw data enabled us to start writing our insights about common and unique user needs and pain points.

We found out that most of our target users have the same issues in the long run during our user interviews, in fact we were surprised at how common were these problems across different types of users that have different needs.

 

Pain Points

We collected the most common issues users faced as our main pain points for our first phase of project implementation and lunch, these common pain points were:

1

Forcing sign up

Problem statement

Users can’t explorer the app before adding their personal data and create an account

Our solution assumption

In our app creating an account will be only required during the checkout process

2

No shortcuts

Problem statement

Users can’t order from just one page they have to go through multiple pages before the checkout every time, for busy users crucial

Our solution assumption

Well crafted Home page is all our users will need they can one-tap order and go to every corner of the app from just the home page

3

Location out of range

Problem statement

After users fill their orders they move to location selection page when they find out that their location is not in the delivery range

Our solution assumption

By putting location selection at the start we will 100% avoid this issue as users will know from the start if our restaurant has coverage over their area or not before they start ordering

4

No Time To Order

Problem statement

Some users don’t have enough spare time in the middle of work to order what food they like or explore to try new food

Our solution assumption

By adding pre-order schedule users can schedule their orders days before with a specific time and place for each order

Quantitative user research

Based on our insights from user interviews we focus on the most common pain points users faced and what we observed during the research, a user survey is conducted to fill user interviews gaps and to evaluate conducted pain points with over 100 participants had taken part in the research we found that 40% of our participants tend to order the same food they ordered even if they want to try some other just to Speed up the order process and 55% (including the past 40%) usually order the same food over and over just because they like it and because it’s a faster process that way. Based on that we cast our focus more on Time On Task (TOT) in other words speeding up the order process by eliminating as many unnecessary steps as possible.
